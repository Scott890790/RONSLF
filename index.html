Cybersecurity Blog

1. What is a Cyberattack?

A cyberattack is any offensive maneuver that targets computer information systems, infrastructures, or personal devices. Attackers use various methods to steal data, disable systems, or gain unauthorized access.

2. Why are they dangerous?

The consequences range from financial loss and identity theft to the total disruption of essential services like hospitals or power grids. Once your data is leaked, it often stays on the dark web forever.

3. How to Prevent Them

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Always use an extra layer of security beyond just a password. This prevents 99% of bulk attacks.

Software Updates

Developers release patches to fix security holes. If you don't update, you leave the door open for hackers.

Phishing Awareness

Never click on links in suspicious emails. Always check the sender's actual email address before trusting it.
